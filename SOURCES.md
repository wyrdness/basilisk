# Sources & References

## Ancient & Classical Sources

### Roman
- Pliny the Elder. (77 CE). *Natural History*, Book VIII, Chapter 33; Book XXIX, Chapter 19. First detailed description of basilisk.
- Lucan. (65 CE). *Pharsalia*, Book IX. Basilisk in Libya.
- Aelian. (c. 200 CE). *On the Nature of Animals*, Book II. Basilisk characteristics.
- Solinus. (3rd century CE). *Collectanea Rerum Memorabilium*. Basilisk description.

### Greek
- Pseudo-Apollodorus. (1st-2nd century CE). *Bibliotheca*. References to serpent kings.

## Medieval Sources

### Bestiaries
- *Physiologus* (c. 2nd-4th century CE). Early Christian bestiary with basilisk entry.
- *Aberdeen Bestiary* (c. 12th century). Illuminated manuscript with basilisk illustration.
- *Bodleian Library MS. Ashmole 1511* (13th century). English bestiary.
- *Harley Bestiary* (c. 1230-1240). British Library MS Harley 4751.

### Medieval Encyclopedias & Natural Histories
- Isidore of Seville. (c. 600 CE). *Etymologiae*, Book XII. Encyclopedic basilisk entry.
- Bartholomaeus Anglicus. (c. 1240). *De Proprietatibus Rerum* (On the Properties of Things).
- Vincent of Beauvais. (c. 1250). *Speculum Naturale* (Mirror of Nature).
- Albertus Magnus. (13th century). *De Animalibus* (On Animals).

### Medieval Literature
- Chaucer, Geoffrey. (c. 1380). *The Canterbury Tales*. Basilisk references.
- Various medieval romance literature and bestiaries.
